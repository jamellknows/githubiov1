The Riemann Hypothesis

The Riemann Hypothesis is an 160 year old unsolved mathematics problem first proposed by Bernhard Riemann in 1859. It relates the distribution of prime numbers to the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann Zeta Function and states that they all lie at a 1⁄2.

The P vs NP Problem

The P vs NP problem is the most recent of the Millenium Prize Problems and is in the field of computing. It asks whether every problem eaisly verifiable in polynomial time is easily solvable in polynomial time.

The Acceleration of Inifite Time Averaging of Chaotic Systems

The acceleration of infinite time averaging of chatoic systems is a topic involving the finding of the fastset and most efficient methods to calculatae the infinite time average of chaotic systems. Chaotic Systems are systems considered to be unstable, as they never stop moving. Finding the infite time average of these systems is of great interest to those who study these systems.

The Birch Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture

The Birch Swinnerton Conjecture is a mathematical conjecture about the set of rational points on an ellipse. Better said, it describes the set of rational solutions to the equations describing a curve.
